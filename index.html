History of Rubik's code

In 1974, Hungarian architecture professor Ernő Rubik created a prototype of a three-dimensional cube to help his students understand spatial concepts.

He obtained a Hungarian patent for the puzzle in 1975 and initially called it the “Magic Cube”.

By 1977, the Magic Cube was being mass-produced and had become very popular in Hungary.

In 1980, the puzzle was rebranded as the “Rubik’s Cube” and launched internationally by Ideal Toys.

In 1982, the first World Rubik’s Cube Championship was held in Budapest, with 16-year-old Minh Thai claiming the title in 22.95 seconds.

Between 1980 and 1983, an estimated 100–200 million cubes were sold worldwide, and as of early 2024, around 500 million Rubik’s Cubes have been sold in total.
